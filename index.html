These are our thoughts regarding our ideal jobs after researching this data.

 

Sulaiman Dayoub

Looking at the Burning Glass data has opened my eyes to the popularity of Solutions Architect and Graphics Designer and how in demand they are. However this hasn’t changed my mind on my dream job as my job is still high in demand (Network Engineer) and is personally more interesting over the other alternatives on the list. Systems engineer did pique my interests seeing it 4th from the top, however the likelihood of staying with the Network dream is still high as the skill-set required for that job relates to me more than that of Systems Engineer.




Jeiel Roco

I always had my mind set on a job that would net a good amount of money and would be interesting for me. Upon seeing the Burning Glass data i noticed that my ideal job choice (Software Engineer) was sitting around the middle on the list (11th). This has made me consider looking into Java Developing as well since it relates to my initial job. However I feel that the broader skill-set available in Software Engineering as well as the high potential for income make my initial ideal dream job the one that I will pursue.

 

Danny Nguyen

I really wanted to pursue a job that tied in with augmented reality as I found that so interesting to me. However upon viewing the Burning Glass data I noticed that being a Java developer is high in demand and ties closely with the skill-set required for developing A.R. This has made me solidify my ideal job being a Java developer with aspirations to pursue a final job in the augmented reality sector.

 

Andreas Tan

My Ideal job is currently being a Systems administrator and whilst looking through the Burning Glass data i noticed that the job was sitting comfortably at 8th place on the list. Although I did look into Systems Engineer after seeing its ranking, I ultimately settled for the System administrator role as the skill-set appealed to me more than the System Engineer role.



Steven Saing

My interest in Web Development is what made me come to RMIT, after looking at the Burning Glass data I haven’t changed my opinion much. While the Web Developer title is pretty low on the list, at #15, this hasn’t completely dissuaded me from pursuing this position especially since positions in IT can be rather volatile and Web Development could have a greater demand in the future. The other job titles that are above Web Developer are not that appealing to me so I’m still sticking with my original choice.

 

Frank Morrison

After researching the most recent Burning Glass data I was interested by the high ranking of the Graphic Designer title. Compared to my listed ideal job of Ux Designer, it is much higher on the list, sitting at #2, while Ux Designer is closer to the bottom at #21. This has influenced my opinion for my ideal job as there is such a large discrepancy between the 2 and both are appealing to me and require similar skill-sets. This data has lead me to change my focus towards Graphic Design, however, due to the similar skill-sets if the rankings change dramatically, as is likely for IT positions, I should be able to pivot back to my original choice of Ux Designer with relative ease.
